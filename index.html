General Info

Recycling refers to turning waste into a usable material. Most large cities distribute recycling bins for residents. The following materials CAN be recycled:

Metals, paper/cardboard, plastics, glass, batteries/bulbs, electronics

Explore the waste management website to find recycling drop-off locations near you and learn more about recycling!

Composting food scraps can be beneficial but if done incorrectly it can be really hazardous if you’re misinformed and add things that aren’t supposed to be there. Refer to the guide below to make sure you’re composting the right way: How to Compost Correctly

Upcycle: reuse (discarded objects or material) in such a way as to create a product of a higher quality or value than the original.

Thrift Shop: a store selling secondhand clothes and other household goods, typically to raise funds for a charitable institution.

Consignment Shop: a store that sells second-hand items (typically clothing and accessories) on behalf of the original owner, who receives a percentage of the selling price.

Clothing Options

Hair Towel

Before tossing your shirt, consider turning it into a hair towel. Extra points for a 100% cotton t-shirt, as the cotton will dry your hair thoroughly with less damage than a conventional towel.

Make a Reusable Bag/Tote

Instead of buying a reusable tote for your grocery items, you can turn your shirts into produce bags. Save money and be a little kinder to the environment with this method.

Homemade Feminine Hygiene Products

Instead of using disposable feminine hygiene products that are seriously awful for the environment, consider cutting up your shirt and using that instead. There are actually cloth menstrual pads on the market, but why spend money when you have the resources to do these things for free right in your home. (Women have been doing this since forever, so you shouldn’t be giving money to a company that isn’t doing anything you can’t do yourself).

Make a Pillowcase

If you have silk or satin clothes that you don’t want to wear anymore, do your hair a favor and turn them into a pillowcase. Silk and satin are better for your hair because they allow your hair to retain it’s natural oils and also causes less friction while you toss and turn.


Different Ways to Dispose of Food Waste

Using Food Scraps as Fertilizer

You can use fruit and vegetables scraps such as leaves, roots, and peels to grow your own produce and keep the circle of life going. You can also add in egg shells, rice, grains, or coffee grounds into the mix. For more ideas check out this useful article: Using Food Scrapes in Your Garden

Save Your Citrus Peels!

You can add flavor to any dish by grating citrus peels into a fine zest. You can also candy the peels to make a delicious treat! Learn how to make a candied lemon peel here: Candied Lemon Peel You can even add citrus peels to your water pitcher to freshen things up!

Whiten Teeth With Banana Peels

Before you throw that banana peel away, think about incorporating it into your daily routine! Banana peels contain a natural whitening agent and aren’t as abrasive on your teeth as your average teeth whitener. Either before or after brushing, rub the peel on your teeth for about two minutes and let the peel do its magic!

The Many Uses of Coffee Grounds

You can deodorize your refrigerator by placing a bowl of dry coffee grounds to neutralize the odor. Put coffee grounds on a cleaning rag and watch as it wipes through grime and grease! You can also use coffee grounds in your garden as a protective barrier around plants to ward off insects.


Don't Get Rid of Your Bottles Just Yet!

Learn to Upcycle Plastic Bottles

From planters and sprinklers to pencil holders this site has a ton of creative ways to upcycle plastic water bottles: DIY Plastic Bottle Crafts

Get Decorative With Glass Bottles

Glass bottles can be repurposed into a variety of decorative pieces for your home such as candy jars, flower vase, or, if you’re good with your hands, a glass bottle candelabra. Here is a wonderful resource for upcycling glass products: Reuse Your Glass Bottles

Reuse Aluminum Bottles

Aluminum cans are a perfect way to practice your metal work skills! They are durable and relatively simple to make totally new creations like belts and homemade christmas ornaments. Check out 8 Ways to Reuse Your Cans

If these tips don’t appeal to you, all of these are completely recyclable materials! Refer to our General Info page to learn how to recycle responsibly.



Minimize Your Paper Waste

Keep Your Home Warm

You can burn a fire using tightly rolled newspaper instead of using a starter log. You can also use old newspapers to add some kitschy style to your present wrapping or dip it into a mixture of water and vinegar to wash your windows for a streakless clean!

Make a Pinata

Paper maché piñatas are a Mexican tradition that has been adopted by mainstream american culture. This is a simple project that is sure to be a hit at birthday parties and it’s a great way to reuse paper products! Check out this YouTube Pinata Tutorial to start out!

More Ways to Reuse Paper Materials

Shredded newspaper or junk mail can serve as a perfect cage liner for your beloved pets. The same newspaper, junkmail, or even magazines can be turned into origami or beautiful paper breads.


about us

The central issue that GreenNew is tackling is consumerism and throw-away culture in the United States. Our main goal is to encourage ethical and sustainable recycling and upcycling and help conserve everyday items that we usually send to a landfill. We want to find better ways to reuse items before throwing them away and to encourage smarter decisions when disposing of and donating items. Being women of color, it was also important for us to find the ways in which people from developing countries and indigenous communities conserved and explore how we could integrate that limited consumerism culture into American society and potentially create a less materialistic, more sustainable, and safer Earth.
